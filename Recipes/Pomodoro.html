Fettuccine Pomodoro Recipe

Rubbed garlic and tomato mix. A country bread sliced and topped with
different toppings - the evergreen tomato-basil and an inventive.
The classic Italian starter! The key to making the best
It's all in the tomatoes. Because tomatoes are the focus of this simple
Pomodoro recipe, it’s worth it to use ripe, fresh ones. Juicier
tomatoes work, but drier varieties like Roma are really what you’re looking
for. The ultimate goal is to keep your Pomodoro like toast should be dry.

How to Make Fettuccine Pomodoro Recipe



To prepare Fettuccine Pomodoro Recipe:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-low flame.

  2. Cook onion, stirring occasionally, until soft, 10–12 minutes.

  3. Add garlic and cook, stirring, about 3 minutes.

  4. Add red pepper flakes and cook, stirring for a minute.

  5. Increase heat to medium, add puréed tomatoes, and season lightly with salt.

  6. Cook, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens slightly and flavors have come together, 20–25 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in basil.

  7. Set skillet over high heat and stir in reserved pasta cooking liquid to loosen sauce; bring to a boil.

  8. Add pasta and cook, stirring, until sauce coats pasta and pasta is al dente.

  9. Serve garnished with fresh basil sprig and olive powder.

  10. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 cups of all-purpose flour

  11. In a separate bowl, beat 4 large eggs and gradually add 1 cup of vegetable oil.

  12. Slowly add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.

  13. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and bake for 25-30 minutes.

  14. Slice and enjoy your homemade Fettuccine Pomodoro
