Shawarma

Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern street food made with thinly
sliced meat, typically lamb, chicken, or beef, that has
been marinated and roasted on a vertical spit. The meat is shaved
off in thin slices and usually served wrapped in a flatbread such as pita,
along with various toppings and sauces.


To make Shawarma at home, marinate thinly sliced meat in a
flavorful mixture of spices, garlic, lemon juice, and yogurt,
then roast it on a vertical rotisserie or in the oven until
tender and golden brown. Serve the sliced meat in a flatbread with
your choice of toppings and sauces for a delicious
and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Shawarma:

  • 500 grams thinly sliced lamb, chicken, or beef
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Flatbreads (such as pita or lavash)
  • Sliced tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers
    for serving
  • Tahini sauce, garlic sauce, or hot sauce
    for serving

How to Make Shawarma



To prepare Shawarma:

  1. In a large bowl, combine minced garlic, lemon juice, plain yogurt,
    olive oil, ground cumin, paprika, ground turmeric, ground
    cinnamon, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well to make
    the marinade.

  2. Add the thinly sliced meat to the marinade and toss until evenly
    coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour,
    or overnight for best results.

  3. If using a vertical rotisserie, thread the marinated meat
    onto the spit and cook according to the manufacturer's instructions
    until golden brown and cooked through. If using an oven, preheat the
    oven to 180°C (350°F) and roast the meat on a baking sheet for 25-30 minutes,
    or until cooked through.

  4. Once cooked, remove the meat from the rotisserie or oven and
    let it rest for a few minutes before slicing
    thinly.

  5. Warm the flatbreads in a skillet or oven. Place a generous
    amount of sliced meat on each flatbread, along with sliced
    tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers.

  6. Drizzle with tahini sauce, garlic sauce, or hot sauce, if desired.
    Roll up the flatbreads tightly and serve immediately.

  7. Enjoy Shawarma hot as a delicious street food-style meal!
