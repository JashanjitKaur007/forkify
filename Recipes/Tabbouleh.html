Tabbouleh

Tabbouleh is a refreshing Middle Eastern salad made with finely
chopped parsley, tomatoes, mint, onion, bulgur wheat, and seasoned
with olive oil, lemon juice, and salt. It is a vibrant and flavorful
dish that is commonly served as a side dish or
mezze (appetizer).


To make Tabbouleh, start by soaking bulgur wheat in water to soften it.
Then, combine the soaked bulgur wheat with finely chopped parsley,
tomatoes, mint, and onion in a bowl. Dress the salad with
olive oil, lemon juice, and salt, and toss until well combined. Chill the
Tabbouleh in the refrigerator before serving to allow the flavors
to meld.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Tabbouleh:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 1 1/2 cups boiling water
  • 2 cups finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped cucumber
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup diced radishes
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

How to Make Tabbouleh



To prepare Tabbouleh:

  1. In a large bowl, place the bulgur wheat and pour the boiling water
    over it. Cover the bowl and let it sit for about 20-30 minutes,
    or until the bulgur is softened and the water is absorbed.

  2. Once the bulgur is ready, fluff it with a fork and let it cool to
    room temperature.

  3. Add the chopped parsley, diced tomatoes, chopped mint leaves, and
    finely chopped onion to the bowl with the bulgur.

  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, and salt.

  5. Pour the dressing over the salad ingredients in the bowl. Toss gently
    to combine, ensuring that the bulgur wheat and vegetables are
    evenly coated with the dressing.

  6. Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt or lemon juice if needed.

  7. If desired, add chopped cucumber, bell pepper, or diced radishes
    to the Tabbouleh for extra flavor and crunch.

  8. Chill the Tabbouleh in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before
    serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

  9. Serve Tabbouleh chilled as a refreshing side dish or appetizer. Enjoy!
