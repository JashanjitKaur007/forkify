Fatayer

Fatayer is a popular Middle Eastern pastry filled with a savory
mixture, typically made with spinach, cheese, or meat. These delicious
pastries are enjoyed as a snack or appetizer and are often
served during special occasions and gatherings.


To make Fatayer, a dough is prepared from flour, yeast, water,
and oil, then rolled out and filled with a flavorful mixture.
The filled dough is then shaped into triangles or small
pies, baked until golden brown, and served warm.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Fatayer:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 cup chopped spinach
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground sumac
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to Make Fatayer



To prepare Fatayer:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it
    sit for about 5 minutes until foamy.

  2. In the meantime, prepare the filling by mixing together chopped
    spinach, crumbled feta cheese, chopped parsley, chopped
    onion, minced garlic, lemon juice, ground sumac (if using), salt,
    and pepper in a bowl.

  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment
    paper.

  4. Punch down the risen dough and divide it into equal-sized balls.
    Roll out each ball into a circle on a floured
    surface.

  5. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle.
    Fold the edges of the dough over the filling to form a triangle or
    boat shape, pinching the edges together
    to seal.

  6. Arrange the stuffed pastries on the prepared baking sheet. Brush the
    tops with olive oil.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

  8. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before serving. Enjoy
    Fatayer warm or at room temperature.
