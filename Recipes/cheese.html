Mexican Street Corn Bake Recipe

Mexican Street Corn Bake is a delightful and flavorful dish that
captures the essence of traditional street corn in an easy-to-serve
bake. It's a crowd-pleaser and perfect for any gathering. We
discovered Mexican street corn at a festival. This easy one-pan
version saves on prep and cleanup. Every August I freeze a lot
of our own fresh sweet corn and then I use that in this recipe,
but store-bought corn works just as well.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Mexican Street Corn Bake:

    • 6 cups frozen corn kernels, thawed
    • 1 cup mayonnaise
    • 1/2 cup sour cream
    • 1 cup crumbled cotija cheese
    • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
    • 1 teaspoon chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
    • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • Juice of 1 lime
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 cup shredded Mexican cheese blend
    • Lime wedges and additional cilantro for garnish
    • 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion
    • 1 jalapeño, seeded and finely chopped

How to Make Mexican Street Corn Bake



To prepare Mexican Street Corn Bake:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, combine thawed corn, mayonnaise, sour creaminess

  3. Cotija cheese, chopped cilantro, red onion, jalapeño, chili powder

  4. Cayenne pepper, smoked paprika, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

  5. Transfer the corn mixture to the prepared baking dish.

  6. Sprinkle shredded Mexican cheese blend evenly over the top.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes

  8. Bake untill the edges are golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.

  10. Garnish with additional cilantro and serve warm with lime wedges on the side.
