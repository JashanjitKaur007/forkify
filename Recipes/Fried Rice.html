Fried Rice

Fried rice is a popular and versatile dish in many Asian cuisines,
known for its simplicity, flavor, and ability to incorporate various ingredients.
It originated in China but has since spread to other parts of Asia and
the world, with each region adding its own twist to the recipe.


The basic components of fried rice include cooked rice, typically leftover
rice that has been cooled to prevent it from sticking together. This rice
is then stir-fried with a mixture of ingredients such as
vegetables, eggs, meat, seafood, and seasonings.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Fried Rice:

  • 3 cups cooked rice
  • 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable or sesame oil)
  • 1 cup carrots
  • 1 cup peas
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (corn,bell pepper and onions)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1/4 cup roasted cashews or peanuts
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

How to Make Fried Rice



To prepare Fried Rice:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.

  2. Add the mixed vegetables to the skillet and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until
    they are tender-crisp.

  3. Add the cooked rice to the skillet and break up any clumps with a
    spatula. Stir-fry for a few minutes to heat the rice through.

  4. In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce and sesame oil.

  5. Pour the sauce over the rice mixture in the skillet and stir well to
    coat everything evenly.

  6. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.

  7. Garnish with chopped green onions before serving.
