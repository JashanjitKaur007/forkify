Lebanese Pilaf

Lebanese Pilaf, also known as "Riz Bi Sh'arieh" in Arabic, is a
traditional Lebanese rice dish cooked with vermicelli
noodles. It is a simple yet flavorful dish often served as a
side dish or as part of a larger meal.


To make Lebanese Pilaf, rice and vermicelli noodles are toasted
in butter or olive oil until golden brown, then cooked with broth
or water until fluffy and tender. The result is a fragrant
and delicious rice dish that pairs well with a
variety of meats and vegetables.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Lebanese Pilaf:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1/2 cup vermicelli noodles
  • 2 tablespoons butter or olive oil
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped almonds or pine nuts
  • 1/4 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 tsp ground turmeric

How to Make Lebanese Pilaf



To prepare Lebanese Pilaf:

  1. In a large skillet or saucepan, heat butter or olive oil over
    medium heat.

  2. Add the vermicelli noodles to the skillet and cook, stirring
    constantly, until golden brown.

  3. Add the rice to the skillet and cook, stirring, for an additional
    1-2 minutes.

  4. Stir in the chicken or vegetable broth and season with salt and
    pepper to taste.

  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover
    the skillet.

  6. Simmer the pilaf for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and
    the liquid is absorbed.

  7. Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with chopped fresh parsley,
    if desired.

  8. Serve Lebanese Pilaf hot as a side dish or as part of a larger meal.
