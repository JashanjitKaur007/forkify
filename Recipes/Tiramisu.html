Tiramisu Cake Recipe

Rubbed garlic and tomato mix. A country bread sliced and topped with
different toppings - the evergreen tomato-basil and an inventive.
The classic Italian starter! The key to making the best Tiramisu Cake?
It's all in the tomatoes. Because tomatoes are the focus of this simple
Tiramisu Cake recipe, it’s worth it to use ripe, fresh ones. Juicier
tomatoes work, but drier varieties like Roma are really what you’re looking
for. The ultimate goal is to keep your Tiramisu Cake like toast should be dry.

How to Make Tiramisu Cake



To prepare Tiramisu Cake:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together 2 cups of all-purpose flour

  3. In a separate bowl, beat 4 large eggs and gradually add 1 cup of vegetable oil.

  4. Slowly add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.

  5. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and bake for 25-30 minutes.

  6. While the cakes are baking, prepare the Tiramisu frosting by beating together.

  7. Once the cakes have cooled, spread a generous layer of Tiramisu frosting.

  8. Optional: Dust the top of the cake with cocoa powder for a finishing touch.

  9. Chill the cake in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.

  10. Slice and enjoy your homemade Tiramisu Cake!
