Mushroom Risotto Recipe

An antipasto dish, Mushroom Risotto has grilled bread topped with veggies,
rubbed garlic and tomato mix. A country bread sliced and topped with
different toppings - the evergreen tomato-basil and an inventive
mushroom-garlic. The classic Italian starter! The key to making the
best Mushroom Risotto? It's all in the tomatoes. Because tomatoes are the focus
of this simple recipe, it’s worth it to use ripe, fresh ones. Juicier tomatoes work,
but drier varieties like Roma are really what you’re looking for.
The ultimate goal is to keep your toast like toast should be dry.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Mushroom Risotto:

    • 1 loaf of country bread
    • Cloves of garlic
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • Basil leaves
    • 5 Tbsp of olive oil
    • 4 portobello mushrooms, sliced
    • 1 garlic clove, grated
    • 1 red chilli, deseeded and chopped
    • 1 Tbsp butter
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • 4 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 packet active dry yeast
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1 1/2 cups warm water
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 teaspoon salt

How to Make Mushroom Risotto



To prepare Mushroom Risotto:

  1. Slice the tomatoes and Buffalo Mozzarella.

  2. Arrange tomato and mozzarella slices on a plate, alternating with Cherry Tomatoes and Avocado.

  3. Sprinkle Tulsi and Red Onion on top.

  4. Add green chillies & ginger & cook till saag gets thick.

  5. Drizzle 1/4 cup Balsamic Vinegar over the salad. Add Salt and Pepper to taste.

  6. Optionally, garnish with additional Tulsi leaves.

  7. In a food processor, combine Basil, Pine nuts, and grated Parmesan Cheese.

  8. Pulse until the ingredients are finely chopped.

  9. With the processor running, gradually add Extra Virgin Olive Oil.

  10. Continue blending until the mixture forms a smooth paste.

  11. Squeeze the Juice of half a Lemon into the pesto.

  12. Add salt to taste (typically around 1/2 teaspoon).
