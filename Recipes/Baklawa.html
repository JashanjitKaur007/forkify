Baklava

Baklava is a sweet and rich Middle Eastern dessert made with layers of
phyllo pastry, chopped nuts, and honey or syrup. It is known for its
flakytexture and sweet, nutty flavor, making it a favorite treat
for special occasions and celebrations.


To make Baklava, layers of buttered phyllo pastry are stacked with a
filling of chopped nuts, such as walnuts, pistachios, or almonds,
and then baked until golden brown. Once baked, the Baklava is
soaked in a sweet syrup made with sugar, water, and lemon juice
or flavored with rose water or orange blossom water.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Baklava:

  • 1 package (16 ounces) phyllo pastry sheets, thawed
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped nuts (such as walnuts,
    pistachios, or almonds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • orange blossom water or rose water for
    flavoring
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional: additional spices such as
    cardamom or nutmeg

How to Make Baklava



To prepare Baklava:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. In a bowl, mix together the chopped nuts and ground cinnamon.

  3. Unroll the phyllo pastry sheets and cover them with a damp towel to
    prevent them from drying out.

  4. Place one sheet of phyllo pastry in the prepared baking dish and brush
    it with melted butter. Repeat with several more sheets, brushing each layer
    with butter.

  5. Sprinkle a layer of the chopped nut mixture evenly over the buttered
    phyllo sheets.

  6. Continue layering the phyllo pastry sheets and chopped nuts,
    brushing each layer with melted butter, until all the nuts are used.

  7. Using a sharp knife, carefully cut the Baklava into diamond or
    square shapes.

  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until the Baklava
    is golden brown and crisp.

  9. While the Baklava is baking, prepare the syrup. In a saucepan, combine
    the granulated sugar, water, honey, and lemon juice. Bring to a boil,
    then reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  10. Optional: Add a splash of orange blossom water or rose water to
    the syrup for extra flavor.

  11. Once the Baklava is baked, remove it from the oven and immediately
    pour the hot syrup over the hot Baklava, allowing it to soak in.

  12. Let the Baklava cool completely in the pan before serving.
    Enjoy this delicious Middle Eastern dessert as a sweet treat!
