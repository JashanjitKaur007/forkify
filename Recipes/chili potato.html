Chili Potato

Chili Potato is a popular Indo-Chinese dish that combines crispy
fried potatoes with a spicy and tangy sauce. It's a favorite appetizer
or side dish in many restaurants and households, loved for its addictive
flavor and crunchy texture.


The dish typically features thinly sliced potatoes that are deep-fried until
golden and crispy. They are then tossed in a flavorful sauce made with
ingredients like soy sauce, chili sauce, garlic, ginger, and vinegar.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Chili Potato:

  • 2 large potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsps cornstarch
  • Oil for deep frying
  • For the sauce:
  • 2 tbsps soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp chili sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (capsicum)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped spring onions

How to Make Chili Potato



To prepare Chili Potato:

  1. Coat the thinly sliced potatoes in cornstarch and deep fry until golden
    and crispy. Drain on paper towels and set aside.

  2. In a separate pan, heat a little oil and saut√© the chopped garlic, ginger,
    and green chilies until fragrant.

  3. Add the sliced onions and bell peppers to the pan and cook until they
    are soft.

  4. Stir in the soy sauce, chili sauce, tomato ketchup, and vinegar. Cook for
    a few minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

  5. Add the cornstarch slurry to the sauce and cook until it thickens to
    your desired consistency.

  6. Toss the fried potatoes in the sauce until evenly coated.

  7. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot.
