Dim Sum Recipe

Dim sum is a delightful culinary tradition originating from China,
consisting of small, bite-sized portions of food served in steamer baskets
or on small plates. It is often enjoyed as a communal meal with
family and friends, especially during weekend brunches
or special occasions.


The term "dim sum" translates to "touch the heart" in Cantonese, reflecting
the idea of enjoying small, flavorful dishes that are meant to tantalize
the taste buds and bring joy to the heart.

How to Make Dim Sum



To Prepare Dim Sum:

  1. Visit a dim sum restaurant or prepare dim sum at home using traditional
    recipes.

  2. Choose a variety of dim sum dishes from the menu or steamer carts, ranging
    from dumplings and buns to rolls and desserts.

  3. Share the dim sum dishes with your dining companions, trying a little
    bit of everything.

  4. Pair dim sum with traditional condiments such as soy sauce, chili oil,
    or vinegar for added flavor.

  5. Enjoy the communal dining experience and savor the delicious flavors of
    each dim sum dish.
