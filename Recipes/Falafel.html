Falafel

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground
chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices, then
formed into balls or patties and deep-fried until
crispy and golden brown. It is commonly served in pita bread with
tahini sauce, salad, and pickles.


To make falafel, simply blend all the ingredients together until
smooth, then shape the mixture into balls or
patties and fry until crispy. Serve hot with your favorite
accompaniments.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Falafel:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Pita bread,salad, and pickles for serving

How to Make Falafel



To prepare Falafel:

  1. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley,
    cilantro, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, black
    pepper, flour, and baking powder.

  2. Blend until smooth, scraping down the sides of the processor as needed.

  3. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour
    to firm up.

  4. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties, using wet hands
    to prevent sticking.

  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or skillet to 350°F (180°C).

  6. Fry the falafel in batches until golden brown and crispy, about
    4-5 minutes per batch.

  7. Remove falafel from the oil and drain on paper towels.

  8. Serve hot falafel in pita bread with tahini sauce, salad, and pickles.
