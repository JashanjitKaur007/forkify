Sesameseed Balls

Sesame Balls, also known as Jian Dui or Jin Deui, are popular
Chinese pastries made from glutinous rice flour and filled with sweet bean
paste or lotus seed paste. They are coated in sesame seeds and
deep-fried until crispy on the outside while remaining chewy and soft on
the inside.


These delightful treats are often enjoyed as a snack or dessert during
festivals such as the Lunar New Year or the Mid-Autumn Festival. Their
golden brown exterior and nutty aroma make them a beloved indulgence
for both children and adults alike.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Sesame Balls:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Sweet bean paste
  • lotus seed paste
  • Sesame seeds
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • Additional sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1/4 cup shredded coconut
  • 1 tablespoon honey or syrup
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon powder

How to Make Sesame Balls



To prepare Sesame Balls:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine glutinous rice flour, sugar, and water to form
    a smooth dough.

  2. Divide the dough into small portions and flatten each portion into a circle.

  3. Place a small amount of sweet bean paste or lotus seed paste in the
    center of each dough circle.

  4. Wrap the dough around the filling to form a ball, ensuring that the
    filling is completely enclosed.

  5. Roll each ball in sesame seeds until evenly coated.

  6. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Carefully add the sesame
    balls to the hot oil and fry until they are golden brown and crispy.

  7. Remove the sesame balls from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove
    excess oil.

  8. Let the sesame balls cool slightly before serving. Enjoy them warm or at
    room temperature.
