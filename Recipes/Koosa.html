Stuffed Koosa Squash

Stuffed Koosa Squash, known as "Koosa Mahshi" in Arabic, is a
traditional Middle Eastern dish consisting of hollowed-out
zucchini or squash filled with a savory mixture of rice, meat,
and spices. It is a flavorful and hearty dish that is often
served as a main course.


To make Stuffed Koosa Squash, small zucchini or squash are hollowed
out and stuffed with a mixture of cooked rice, ground meat
(such as lamb or beef), onions, pine nuts, tomatoes, parsley,
and spices. The stuffed squash are then cooked in a tomato-based sauce
until tender and flavorful.

Ingredients:


    Ingredients for Stuffed Koosa Squash:

  • 6 small koosa squash or zucchini
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 250 grams ground meat
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups tomato sauce or diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins

How to Make Stuffed Koosa Squash



To prepare Stuffed Koosa Squash:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Wash the koosa squash or zucchini and trim off the ends.
    Slice each squash in half lengthwise, then use a spoon to hollow
    out the centers, leaving a shell about 1/4 inch thick.
    Reserve the flesh for the filling.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, ground meat,
    chopped onion, chopped tomatoes, pine nuts, chopped parsley,
    ground cinnamon, ground allspice, salt, and pepper. Mix
    well to combine.

  4. Stuff each hollowed-out squash with the rice and meat mixture,
    pressing down gently to pack it in.

  5. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Arrange the
    stuffed squash in the skillet, cut side up.

  6. In a separate bowl, mix together the tomato sauce or diced
    tomatoes and chicken or vegetable broth. Pour the mixture
    over the stuffed squash.

  7. Cover the skillet with a lid or aluminum foil and transfer it
    to the preheated oven.

  8. Bake for 45-60 minutes, or until the squash are tender and the
    filling is cooked through.

  9. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes before serving.

  10. Serve Stuffed Koosa Squash hot, garnished with additional chopped
    parsley if desired.
